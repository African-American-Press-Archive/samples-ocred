The Fran Frans, one of the pioneer ladies' social clubs in the city, met with Mrs W. C. Maxwell, 4254 Lucky street on Thursday of last week and disbanded indefinitely. This is the club that, in a spirit of patriotism, when our boys were called to France, abandoned its teutonic name, took up the chase of the Kaiser and was known as the "Thursday Club." The original name was reclaimed after the armistice.

In harmony with its many past activities for charity, the members donated the final contents of its treasury, $17.00, to the People's Hospital.

Y. M. C. A. BOY'S DIVISION OPENS FALL PROGRAM

The first fall class in Pine Street Y. M. C. A. Boy's Division and Elliardsville Y. M. C. A. Club for Boys will be held Monday October 2nd. This day will be in the finest program for boys ever offered by these Departments and one of the finest four-fold programs being offered any boys anywhere.

The "Midgets" (under 13 years) will be organized into "Ys Trail Hangers" the "Juniora" will be organized into Tribes of "Pioneers" with the world famous Christian Citizenship Training Program to guide it. Both programs will consist of handcraft, scout craft, woodcraft, camp craft, and intellectual, physical, social and religious activities.

The Seniors (16 to 18) will have a great time in their Senior Triangle Club. They will have three gymnastics and swimming classes a class in "Older Boys Problems and Christian Principles", health classes conducted by physletans, orchestra, gymtanks, basket ball, hiking, camping and other fine features.

Many other clubs will be formed later in the Fall. The Summer Older Boys Triangle Club is among them.

The boys will have a big part in the opening exhibition on Friday, October 6th, and they hope to see all of their friends and parents out.

The Cafeteria

The increased demand on our Cafeteria makes it necessary for us to consider possible expansion. A special meeting of the board of Directors is called tonight to discuss plans for an additional service department. It will be of interest to report that the receipts for the past month have been the largest of any previous month and last Sunday, was the largest day on record. With it a very short time we hope to be able to accommodate our patrons in a much larger way.

REV. J. K. PARKER RETURNS
FROM TRAVELS IN SOUTH

Rev. J. K. Parker has returned from his trip south where he had gone to attend the funeral of the late Dr. E. C. Morris, after which he visted Greenville and Luhi, Miss, and Memphis, Tenn.

The pastor was very ill with chills and fever while at Greenville. Through the excellent care of Rev. Parker's piece, Miss Evelyn Woods, and the attention given him by Rev. Lindsey and the Rev. J. H. Davenport and family, Rev. Parker was brought back to good health again.

The members of Pleasant Green Baptist Church wish to thank these friends through the Argus for the kindness shown their pastor while he was in a distant land.

Pleasant Green will hold its fall revival October 4th to November 5, 1922. The allied forces of the Kingdom of Christ are called upon to rally to this battlefield to help win a victory for our Lord.

Prayer service will be going on night and day during the revival services. Each soldier is asked to meet at the church every Sunday morning between the hours of 5 a. m. and 7:20 a. m. to receive sufficient grace to put over this program for God and his Christ.

The Fran Frans, one of the pioneer ladies' social clubs in the city, met with Mrs W. C. Maxwell, 4254 Lucky street on Thursday of last week and disbanded indefinitely. This is the club that, in a spirit of patriotism, when our boys were called to France, abandoned its teutonic name, took up the chase of the Kaiser and was known as the "Thursday Club." The original name was reclaimed after the armistice. In harmony with its many past activities for charity, the members donated the final contents of its treasury, $17.00, to the People's Hospital.

Y. M. C. A. BOY'S DIVISION OPENS FALL PROGRAM

The first fall class in Pine Street Y. M. C. A. Boy's Division and Elliardsville Y. M. C. A. Club for Boys will be held Monday October 2nd. This day will be in the finest program for boys ever offered by these Departments and one of the finest four-fold programs being offered any boys anywhere. The "Midgets" (under 13 years) will be organized into "Ys Trail Hangers" the "Juniora" will be organized into Tribes of "Pioneers" with the world famous Christian Citizenship Training Program to guide it. Both programs will consist of handcraft, scout craft, woodcraft, camp craft, and intellectual, physical, social and religious activities. The Seniors (16 to 18) will have a great time in their Senior Triangle Club. They will have three gymnastics and swimming classes a class in "Older Boys Problems and Christian Principles", health classes conducted by physletans, orchestra, gymtanks, basket ball, hiking, camping and other fine features. Many other clubs will be formed later in the Fall. The Summer Older Boys Triangle Club is among them. The boys will have a big part in the opening exhibition on Friday, October 6th, and they hope to see all of their friends and parents out. REV. J. K. PARKER RETURNS FROM TRAVELS IN SOUTH

Rev. J. K. Parker has returned from his trip south where he had gone to attend the funeral of the late Dr. E. C. Morris, after which he visted Greenville and Luhi, Miss, and Memphis, Tenn. The pastor was very ill with chills and fever while at Greenville. Through the excellent care of Rev. Parker's piece, Miss Evelyn Woods, and the attention given him by Rev. Lindsey and the Rev. J. H. Davenport and family, Rev. Parker was brought back to good health again. The members of Pleasant Green Baptist Church wish to thank these friends through the Argus for the kindness shown their pastor while he was in a distant land. Pleasant Green will hold its fall revival October 4th to November 5, 1922. The allied forces of the Kingdom of Christ are called upon to rally to this battlefield to help win a victory for our Lord. Prayer service will be going on night and day during the revival services. Each soldier is asked to meet at the church every Sunday morning between the hours of 5 a. m. and 7:20 a. m. to receive sufficient grace to put over this program for God and his Christ.
