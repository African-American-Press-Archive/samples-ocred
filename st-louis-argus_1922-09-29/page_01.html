The Association had filed a suit to enjoin Carter from moving into the flat on the ground that the block was restricted against Colored people, and the attorney for the defendant filed their return in the matter, the attorney for the plaintiff saw he had no case and dismissed the case, and the keys have been turned over to the owner, Elmer J. Carter.

The case attracted a deal of attention, and the victory on the part of Carter is regarded as a victory for the race, because many of the so-called Protective Association have sprung up in different parts of the city.
