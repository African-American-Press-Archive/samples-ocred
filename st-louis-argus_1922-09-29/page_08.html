YOU ARE ALWAYS PROUD OF YOUR TOWN

YOU ARE ALWAYS PROUD OF YOUR TOWN

Preston News Service:
Most people boast of the things produced in their town. Did you ever stop to think of the thing in your town which does more to show the real progress you are making than anything else? If you have, we are sure that you soon found out that it was your newspaper. The way for an outsider to tell just how live and active the people of a town are is by observing their newspaper. If it is full of advertising, brimfull of news of progressive activity among our people; contains strong race achievement; and news of racial activity for betterment and development, you can bet your sweet life that the people of the town are wideawake. The editor cannot do this alone. You business men must give him your advertisements and pay for them; the readers must pay their subscriptions, and everybody must let the editor know what he or she is doing for race progress. Stand by your paper in a co-operative manner and let the world know you are doing big things in your town.

NATIONAL FOODS ARE AN INTERESTING STUDY

NATIONAL FOODS ARE AN INTERESTING STUDY

Associated Negro Press.
NEW HAVEN, Conn. Sept. 27.—"What people eat is becoming a most important question." declares the Scientist of Yale University in a recent discussion of this matter. Continuing this authority states:
"National foods are a most interesting study. Scotland's food is cats, taken in "parrish" form. Why oats? Because they are heating and Scotland is a cool country, Ireland's national food is the potato. Why? Because Irish soil suits its cultivation. England's national food is roast beef. Why? Well—the English were ever a breechening race and the fighter always required red meat.
"The Hindu's natural food is rice, because it supplies in small form an extraordinary amount of energy and staying power. That is exactly what a hot country requires.
"The national food of Germany is pork—in any form—because pork is always the chosen food of the clever and stern.
"Our French friends have no outstanding national food but are the greatest vegetables in the world. That unfortunately, is a sign of loss of national virility. Historians and scientists have commented on the fact when investigating the decline and fall of nations.
"Italians like oily foods. That is they are not great meat eaters, and they make up the deficiency of protein and carbohydrates with oil.
"Russia's national food is rye. That is due to economic circumstances. It is easy to grow, cheap, and nourishing. It has been called "serf food," and that explains much.
"America has national food; but it is queer to note how universal is the demand for prepared food, quickly eaten and quickly digested. That indicates the "nation in a hurry."
As nothing is said to distinguish the Negro from the other American elements, it is a fair presumption to feel that, despite the "color problems" the colored man and brother is also "in a hurry."

LESSON FOR OCTOBER 1

BIRTH OF JOHN THE BAPTIST

REFERENCE MATERIAL-Isa. 40.3-5:
Mal. 3:4-6

PRIMARY TOPIC-An Angel Brings
Good News

JUNIOR TOPIC—The Birth of John the Baptist.
INTERMEDIATE AND SENIOR TOPIC—Proportion for the Congregation of Christ.

INTERMEDIATE AND SENIOR TOPIC
- Preparation for the Coming of Christ.
- YOUNG PEOPLE AND ADULT TOPIC
- The Place and Work of John the Baptist.

We are today entering upon a six months' course of study in the Gospel of Luke, whose general subject is "Jesus the World's Savior." Today's lesson concerns the birth of John the Baptist, the forerunner of Christ.

I. The Parents of John the Baptist (iv, 5.7)

1. The Parents of John the Baptist (vv. 5-7).

1. When They Lived (v. 5). "In the days of Herod the King." They lived in a day when ungodliness was rife. The priesthood was very corrupt and the ruling classes were wicked. Zacharias and his wife lived in a time when it was not so easy to be godly.

2. Their Character (v. 6). They were righteous before God. To be righteous before God is a high tribute. Many appear to be righteous before men who are not righteous before God. Their lives were so mated that they walked in the commandments of the Lord blameless. How beautiful it is when husband and wife are united in the Lord and walk together in fellowship with God.

3. They Were Childless (v. 7). Though this godly couple were well mated and they possessed the joy of the Lord in their souls, there was a real lack in that home.

II. The Birth of John Promised (vv. 8-17).

1. By Whom (vv. 11; cf. v. 19). The angel Gabriel appeared and made known the good news to Zacharias. This exalted being, the special messenger of God, was sent to make this disclosure.

2. When (vv. 8-14). While officiating as priest this good news came to him. Zacharias must have been definitely praying about this very matter (v. 13). As he burned incense, which typified prayer, the multitude without were praying.

3. Characteristics of the Child (vv. 15-17). (1) Shall be great in the sight of the Lord (v. 15). Though the people did not greatly esteem him he was highly esteemed by the Lord. This is infinitely better than if he had been greatly esteemed in the eyes of men or in his own eyes. This is the esteem for which we all should long and pray. (2) Shall drink neither wine nor strong drink (v. 15). The child shall become a Nazarite, separating himself from the sensuous things and dedicating himself to the service of the Lord. (3) Be filled with the Holy Ghost (v. 15). The energy of the divine Spirit would enable him to lead the people to repentance (v. 16). (4) Shall go in the spirit and power of Elijah (v. 17). In this power he was to prepare the people for the coming of the Savior and the salvation which He was to bring.

III. Zacharias Asking for a Sign (vv. 18-23).

Although the aged priest was earnestly praying for the salvation of Israel the gracious promise of the angel, which was the beginning of that salvation, staggered his faith. He was unable to believe that that for which he fondly hoped and prayed would be realized. The angel gave Zacharias a sign. He was smitten with dumbrex which was to continue until the fulfillment of the promise. Because he refused to praise God in faith for this gracious promise God caused his tongue to be silent until the promise was fulfilled and his lips could open in thanksgiving and praise. Though he was thus rebuked, at the same time his faith was strengthened by the manifestation of the supernatural God wants us to trust Him, to believe his promises, no matter how contrary to reason they may seem.

IV. The Promise Fulfilled (vv. 57-63).

When the time came for the birth of John, Elizabeth brought forth a son and the neighbors rejoiced with her. On the eighth day they circumcised the child and gave him a name according to the instruction of the angel (v. 13). The name John was contrary to the family custom. By means of writing Zacharias made known the name which he would have by divine instruction. At this time God opened the mouth of Zacharias and he offered up praise.

YOU ARE ALWAYS PROUD OF YOUR TOWN

YOU ARE ALWAYS PROUD OF YOUR TOWN

Preston News Service: Most people boast of the things produced in their town. Did you ever stop to think of the thing in your town which does more to show the real progress you are making than anything else? If you have, we are sure that you soon found out that it was your newspaper. The way for an outsider to tell just how live and active the people of a town are is by observing their newspaper. If it is full of advertising, brimfull of news of progressive activity among our people; contains strong race achievement; and news of racial activity for betterment and development, you can bet your sweet life that the people of the town are wideawake. The editor cannot do this alone. You business men must give him your advertisements and pay for them; the readers must pay their subscriptions, and everybody must let the editor know what he or she is doing for race progress. Stand by your paper in a co-operative manner and let the world know you are doing big things in your town.

NATIONAL FOODS ARE AN INTERESTING STUDY

NATIONAL FOODS ARE AN INTERESTING STUDY

Associated Negro Press. NEW HAVEN, Conn. Sept. 27.—"What people eat is becoming a most important question." declares the Scientist of Yale University in a recent discussion of this matter. Continuing this authority states: "National foods are a most interesting study. Scotland's food is cats, taken in "parrish" form. Why oats? Because they are heating and Scotland is a cool country, Ireland's national food is the potato. Why? Because Irish soil suits its cultivation. England's national food is roast beef. Why? Well—the English were ever a breechening race and the fighter always required red meat. "The Hindu's natural food is rice, because it supplies in small form an extraordinary amount of energy and staying power. That is exactly what a hot country requires. "The national food of Germany is pork—in any form—because pork is always the chosen food of the clever and stern. "Our French friends have no outstanding national food but are the greatest vegetables in the world. That unfortunately, is a sign of loss of national virility. Historians and scientists have commented on the fact when investigating the decline and fall of nations. "Italians like oily foods. That is they are not great meat eaters, and they make up the deficiency of protein and carbohydrates with oil. "Russia's national food is rye. That is due to economic circumstances. It is easy to grow, cheap, and nourishing. It has been called "serf food," and that explains much. "America has national food; but it is queer to note how universal is the demand for prepared food, quickly eaten and quickly digested. That indicates the "nation in a hurry." As nothing is said to distinguish the Negro from the other American elements, it is a fair presumption to feel that, despite the "color problems" the colored man and brother is also "in a hurry." LESSON FOR OCTOBER 1

BIRTH OF JOHN THE BAPTIST

REFERENCE MATERIAL-Isa. 40.3-5: Mal. 3:4-6

PRIMARY TOPIC-An Angel Brings Good News

JUNIOR TOPIC—The Birth of John the Baptist. INTERMEDIATE AND SENIOR TOPIC—Proportion for the Congregation of Christ.

INTERMEDIATE AND SENIOR TOPIC - Preparation for the Coming of Christ. - YOUNG PEOPLE AND ADULT TOPIC - The Place and Work of John the Baptist. We are today entering upon a six months' course of study in the Gospel of Luke, whose general subject is "Jesus the World's Savior." Today's lesson concerns the birth of John the Baptist, the forerunner of Christ. I. The Parents of John the Baptist (iv, 5.7)

1. The Parents of John the Baptist (vv. 5-7). 1. When They Lived (v. 5). "In the days of Herod the King." They lived in a day when ungodliness was rife. The priesthood was very corrupt and the ruling classes were wicked. Zacharias and his wife lived in a time when it was not so easy to be godly. 2. Their Character (v. 6). They were righteous before God. To be righteous before God is a high tribute. Many appear to be righteous before men who are not righteous before God. Their lives were so mated that they walked in the commandments of the Lord blameless. How beautiful it is when husband and wife are united in the Lord and walk together in fellowship with God. 3. They Were Childless (v. 7). Though this godly couple were well mated and they possessed the joy of the Lord in their souls, there was a real lack in that home. II. The Birth of John Promised (vv. 8-17). 1. By Whom (vv. 11; cf. v. 19). The angel Gabriel appeared and made known the good news to Zacharias. This exalted being, the special messenger of God, was sent to make this disclosure. 2. When (vv. 8-14). While officiating as priest this good news came to him. Zacharias must have been definitely praying about this very matter (v. 13). As he burned incense, which typified prayer, the multitude without were praying. 3. Characteristics of the Child (vv. 15-17). (1) Shall be great in the sight of the Lord (v. 15). Though the people did not greatly esteem him he was highly esteemed by the Lord. This is infinitely better than if he had been greatly esteemed in the eyes of men or in his own eyes. This is the esteem for which we all should long and pray. (2) Shall drink neither wine nor strong drink (v. 15). The child shall become a Nazarite, separating himself from the sensuous things and dedicating himself to the service of the Lord. (3) Be filled with the Holy Ghost (v. 15). The energy of the divine Spirit would enable him to lead the people to repentance (v. 16). (4) Shall go in the spirit and power of Elijah (v. 17). In this power he was to prepare the people for the coming of the Savior and the salvation which He was to bring. III. Zacharias Asking for a Sign (vv. 18-23). Although the aged priest was earnestly praying for the salvation of Israel the gracious promise of the angel, which was the beginning of that salvation, staggered his faith. He was unable to believe that that for which he fondly hoped and prayed would be realized. The angel gave Zacharias a sign. He was smitten with dumbrex which was to continue until the fulfillment of the promise. Because he refused to praise God in faith for this gracious promise God caused his tongue to be silent until the promise was fulfilled and his lips could open in thanksgiving and praise. Though he was thus rebuked, at the same time his faith was strengthened by the manifestation of the supernatural God wants us to trust Him, to believe his promises, no matter how contrary to reason they may seem. IV. The Promise Fulfilled (vv. 57-63). When the time came for the birth of John, Elizabeth brought forth a son and the neighbors rejoiced with her. On the eighth day they circumcised the child and gave him a name according to the instruction of the angel (v. 13). The name John was contrary to the family custom. By means of writing Zacharias made known the name which he would have by divine instruction. At this time God opened the mouth of Zacharias and he offered up praise.

When lenity and mercy play for a kingdom, the grifter gamester is the soonest winner—Shakespeare. Heaven Often Smites. Heaven often smites in mercy, even when the blow is severest—Joanna Bailille.
