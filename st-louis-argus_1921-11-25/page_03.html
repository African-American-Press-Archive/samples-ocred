500 RENTERS

During the last 10 years some 500 families of people have built or bought their homes in South Kindle Park near Ferguson, Missouri, and are now living in them. They have had more to eat, more fresh vegetables, more poultry, more eggs, more fresh meat, more employment, better sleep and better health with fewer deaths than any other families of the same station. They have been taken to Kindle, empty handed. Some of them have their places fully paid for, others have them partly paid for, and others are just starting. The money they have saved and put into these homes will vary from $200 to $900/each. Perhaps the average amount saved by each of these 500 families will be $600. 500 times $800 is $300,000— thus the owners actually saved $300,000 during the time they have been in Kindle. What have the owners spent their rent paid their rent 12 times each year, and among the 500, they have 600,000 little pieces of paper—rent receipts, worth nothing to them. They probably cost them an average $10,000 each; 60,000 times $10.00 is $300,000— thus the renters have spent and lost $300,000 while the owners saved $300,000. Choose ye this day when ye will serve—yourself or a landlord.

500 RENTERS

During the last 10 years some 500 families of people have built or bought their homes in South Kindle Park near Ferguson, Missouri, and are now living in them. They have had more to eat, more fresh vegetables, more poultry, more eggs, more fresh meat, more employment, better sleep and better health with fewer deaths than any other families of the same station. They have been taken to Kindle, empty handed. Some of them have their places fully paid for, others have them partly paid for, and others are just starting. The money they have saved and put into these homes will vary from $200 to $900/each. Perhaps the average amount saved by each of these 500 families will be $600. 500 times $800 is $300,000— thus the owners actually saved $300,000 during the time they have been in Kindle. What have the owners spent their rent paid their rent 12 times each year, and among the 500, they have 600,000 little pieces of paper—rent receipts, worth nothing to them.
