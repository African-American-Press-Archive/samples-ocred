JACK JOHNSON
SELF-MADE MAN

By Juli Jones, Jr.

Jack Johnson as a self-made man stands out as the greatest individual in history. He is regarded of color or creed. It may be said that the writer has stretched home, but I have no apologies to him, since I am merely a sporting writer.

Jack Johnson started out in life alone to be the world's greatest man in the art of self defense. Out of 90 men he comes a Race man trying to get chance to contest for the great honour of being a champion. Three had been considered—George Goolfrey, Peter Jackson and Jack Johnson. They were all first-class men. They would all three have been world famous, but they had ever had a chance. Unfortunate success. Jackson came in the days of John L. Sullivan, who drew the color line. Why Jack Johnson must be classed as one of the world's greatest fighters was that he knew from the beginning that it took him than skill in the ring to accomplish such a feat that fact was the money. History tells us that John L. Sullivan made a million dollars; also Peter Jackson, but Parson Davies got the better part of money made thousands upon thousands and a portion of his best portion of it. So on down the line with James Corbett with his half million; Jae Cans and Bob Fitzsimmons, who broke. Jim Jeffries only took a little money until he fought James. Jack set out to do what the wise heir had done, but this was possible—that is, for a man, no matter how good a fighter he may be, could not get far without a manager in good standing with matchmakers and sporting editors. That may have been all his money, good men, but Jack broke the rule he was told. He was said that he wont without many meals and stood a lot of hardships with a golden smile to gain his success. He was driven from many training camps because he would not act as scullion because he was not a man. There he was stumped as a coward. Good and would never be anything. It has been said that once he had to stop in the middle of a bout because he was weak from hunger. Things were unmanageable, no manager of reputation could help him. On the other hand, Jack didn't want any of them to handle him.

Jack fought a long time, winning every light until the press started giving him eight or ten lines. He never stopped trying. He stepped into the hollowest room in the California wonder. History will show that Jack knocked out and beat more men than any champion up to this day. He first agitated the sporting whites by trimming Jeffries, their idol. Then cried went over the country that he had knocked out and beat more champions' place? Did he do anything contrary to law? He lived up to the Constitution of the United States, which says all men are born equal and have the same equal rights. The final off came when they medied with his penis in a concert, briefly, that he was a public speaker, should marry and associate with. This question came up with one of the Presidents of the United States when he was asked why he invited a Race gentleman to sit down with his family he was a public speaker, should marry and associate with. This seemed to establish an unwritten law, but in Jack's case it was different: he part that hurts is that he was a public speaker, should marry and associate with. In one particular case one of the best organized clubs in the world of our Race, situated in the middle west, claiming to have more intelligent men than any grown in America, extended an invitation to plain his case of being persecuted on account of his color, before them. This sounded good to Jack, but when he was within he found things different. He meched wound up by a West Indian, and both races got Jack wrong. His aim in life was not to be a doctor, preacher, lawyer or race leader. He set out to be the best man with his fists, which he did beyond all questions. He had been an Indian, Greek, Thai and nationality that his own race would have turned on him? Never.

We cannot find one case where Jack rined any young girl's future or broke his mother's happy home; neither was he mistreated by his parents. Never was he found in the gutter drunk or making a disgraceful fellow of himself, throwing away his money. Jack has been self-esteemed from his country, self-esteemed from England, France, Spain and Mexico tell us that he has conducted himself in the most gentlemanly manner and was a welcomed back in their countries at the invitation of theident of Mexico directed an army officer to make an American white man who conducted an ice cream parlor to a public apology to Jack for some of his bad services which he extended to Jack.

Another case of Jack's mainly behavior in Spain was that some time ago a buyer for one of the largest houses in Chicago, perhaps in the world, who has made his home in Spain for the last five years, a special visit to the South Side where he was here from Spain on a visit and tried to find out why Jack Johnson was made to leave this country. He said that in his personal acquaintance with Jack he had always found him a man who could be proud of. Today Jack Johnson is sonally known men in the world. This statement includes the late Theodore Roosevelt, Burton Holmes, the Kaiser, President Wilson and many more. Jack has shaken more different hands and made more people face to face than the whole layout of the building, the user with every nutton except his own. In closing allow the writer to ask a few questions.
