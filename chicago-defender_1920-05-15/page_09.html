By Juli Jones, Jr.

Jack Johnson as a self-made man stands out as the greatest individual in history. He is regarded of color or creed. It may be said that the writer has stretched home, but I have no apologies to him, since I am merely a sporting writer.

Jack Johnson started out in life alone to be the world's greatest man in the art of self defense. Out of 90 men he comes a Race man trying to get chance to contest for the great honour of being a champion. Three had been considered—George Goolfrey, Peter Jackson and Jack Johnson. They were all first-class men. They would all three have been world famous, but they had ever had a chance. Unfortunate success. Jackson came in the days of John L. Sullivan, who drew the color line. Jack fought a long time, winning every light until the press started giving him eight or ten lines. He never stopped trying. He stepped into the hollowest room in the California wonder. History will show that Jack knocked out and beat more men than any champion up to this day. He first agitated the sporting whites by trimming Jeffries, their idol.
