# "DR. JEKYLL AND MR. HYDE"

Robert Louis Stevenson's powerful story, "Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde," has been translated to the screen with John Barrymore in the leading role and will be shown at the Vendome theater for three days, commencing on Thursday of the present week.

This is declared the most remarkable pictures over filmed, and the performance of Mr. Barrymore, it is claimed, is a worthy companion to that of Richard Mansfield, appeared in the same part in the stage play of Lord Macaulay.

The plot, as is well known, has its central figure a young London physician, Dr. Jekyll, whose tender-heartedness is exceeded only by his all-absorbing interest in his chosen profession. He is an esteemed inocent beautiful girl, Millicent Curew. Her father, Sir George Curew, is a famous man about town. Dr. Jekyll is persuaded by Sir George to accompany him to a low musical hall. From his study of his scientific investigations, he is appointed with a liquid that will separate the good and evil in a man into two distinct bodies. He evolves such a drug, administers it to himself and becomes not only Jekyll, the doctor-shaped, but also Hyde, a misshapen, villian god. Gradually, side of his character overmasters Jekyll, and after attaining dramatic scenes, he succumbs. Only after his death is the dual character of his person revealed.

Northshire man is Mr. Barrymore's leading woman and the picture, which is a Paramount Artery, was directed by John S. Robertson.
