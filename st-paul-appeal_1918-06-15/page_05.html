MASONIC SERMON.

St. John's Day among Masons is one of the notable events of the year, and their annual sermon is usually friends as well as brethren of the order.

This year's celebration will be under the auspices of Anchor Hilyard Lodge No. 2, Minneapolis, and will be held in that city Sunday June 23rd, at Bethesda Baptist church, 11th Ave, and 8th street. Take or transfer to Bloomfield car and get off at 7th and 11th Ave. Rev. D. E. Beasley will preach, and Rev. H. P. Jones, who is expected here, might be present to assist in the ceremonies. Pioneer No. 1 and Perfect Ashlar No. 4 will meet at Union Hall at 1:45 p. m. and go to Minneapolis in a body. The services begin at 2:45 p. m. All are invited to attend.

GOPHER LODGE ELKS.

Holds Regular and Semi-Annual Meeting Wednesday Evening.

Last Wednesday evening, Gopher Lodge 105 I. B. P. O. E. W. held its regular semi-monthly and semi-annual meeting. The attendance was good. Eleven applications for membership were received and L. B. McConnell, J. E. Jackson, Burt Ellis and J. S. Butler were elected to membership.

The secretary and treasurer made their semi-annual reports showing an increase in membership of over 100 per cent and of amount in treasury of over 400 per cent.

The semi-annual and annual elections were held with the following result: F. Coquire E. R.; J. Louis E. I. K. Polk, Mrs. E. L. Orville Turner, E. L. K.; R. M. Johnson, Sec. J. A. Mitchell, Treas.; R. N. Travis, trustee, 3 years; F. B. Simpson, trustee, 1 year.

R. S. Harris and Geo. W. Stewart were elected delegates to the Grand Lodge that meets in Baltimore, Md., in August.

Candidate for Republican Nomination.

The attention of the voters is called particularly to the campaign for Attorney General. Clifford L. Hilton who has been Attorney General since March 8, 1918, and is a candidate for Republican nomination. He has been engaged in the practice of the law nearly thirty years and is now being Attorney General was deputy and assistant attorney general of this state. He was a trusted friend of former Attorney General Smith. Mr. Hilton is opposed by a young man named Sullivan who has the Nonpartisan endorsement. Mr. Sullivan has only been admitted to practice law a little over three years and is thirty-one years of age. It is generally thought that the import office of Attorney General should be left in the hands of an experienced lawyer. The following is an excerpt from the St. Cloud Journal-Press:

Republicans should not overlook the importance of securing the nomination at the primary election of a capable and trustworthy attorney general. Next to that of governor, this is the important office of the state. Clifford L. Hilton, the present attorney general, is especially well qualified for this high office in preparation, training, ability of the character His office is of the Nonpartisan attorneys, and who has been prominent in defending the leaders charged with disloyalty. The attorney general should be a man with courage to enforce the laws of the state, and who is not controlled by any scheming politician or organization. Clifford L. Hilton owe his position only to the people of the state and not to any political boss or league. He can be depended upon to do his duty ally, courageously, and there is no taint of disloyalty in his character. Give him your vote, for the credit of Minnesota.

MINNESOTA STATE FEDERATION.

of Colored Women's Clubs to Meet in St. Paul, June 26-27.

The Thirteenth Annual Convention of the State Federation of Colored Women's Clubs will hold a two days session in St. Paul June 26-27 at Memorial Baptist church, corner of Rice and Fuller streets. Mrs. Mary Taylor, the local chairman, assisted by a splendid committee, has promised to make this convention one of the largest and most interesting in years. Preparations are being made for the sustenance and homes for the delegates and visitors. Plans are also being laid for their special entertainment during their stay in the city.

It is said that no race can rise higher than its women. If this is true how much is expected of us. Let us give all our hopes, prayers, talents and strength, to a noble cause—the uplifting of our race. Our motto is "Lifting as we climb."

The average man or woman, who wins special mention in the battle of life, must fight valiantly, and the colored people are no exception to the rule.

Further announcements will be made.

Yours for our men, women and children.

Estella Dane Wiley,

of Colored Women's Clubs to Meet in St. Paul, June 26-27. The Thirteenth Annual Convention of the State Federation of Colored Women's Clubs will hold a two days session in St. Paul June 26-27 at Memorial Baptist church, corner of Rice and Fuller streets. Mrs. Mary Taylor, the local chairman, assisted by a splendid committee, has promised to make this convention one of the largest and most interesting in years.
