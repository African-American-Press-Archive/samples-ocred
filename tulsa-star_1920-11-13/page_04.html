Affairs in Haiti and Santo Domingo have at last been brought publicly to the attention of the American people the question now is, what is the American people going to do about it? It is not enough to send half a dozen admirals and major generals investigating, with the inevitable result of more or less white-washing reports. The important question is, what shall be American policy toward the Caribbean republics whose independence we have taken from them and whose government is now in the hands of the United States Marine Corps?

Investigation there must be, and consequently punishment but the investigation should be made by Congress and not by interested parties, and the punishment must not be confined to isolated cases of boys, who, taking from their superiors the current attitude of contempt for the natives, were guilty of excesses such as have in all times characterized all armies of occupation lording it over a suppressed people. Nor should penalties stop even with a few brutal刃enants, with possibly a captain or two or a major to give an appearance or thorough expation. The fundamental atrocity of American rule in Haiti and Santo Domingo is the very fact of unjustice.

Nor should penalties stay even with a few brutal neighbors, with possibly a captain or two or a major to give an appearance or thorough expiration. The fundamental atrocity of American rule in Italy and Santo Domingo is the very fact of unlawful execution of unarmed prisoners or brutal torture. It will take a broad-minded committee of the ablest men in Congress—men without color prejudice—to study the question in its largest aspect. There must be no such hasty generalization as characterized that hold Congressional committee which reported on conditions in the virgin Amazon after spending thirty hours there! if such a committee is not appointed by Congress in its coming session the Republicans will convict themselves of having exploited the agony of a subject people merely for campaign material.

There should be both white and Colored Americans among them. Such a joint commission could proceed to the task of holding municipal or local elections, then provincial, where such are in order, and finally to the election of new national assemblies which could replace the caprice of martial law and "executive order" with republican administration.
