at St. Mark's Lyceum
Judge John P. McGorty was the speaker at St. Mark's Lyceum on Sunday, Jan. 16th. While his address was in a Lincolnian line, he spoke of the power of our Race politically and advocated a degree of independence in their voting. The judge was as usual, most prominent. Owing to illness, the musical program was not rendered, in lieu of which Mrs. C. M. B. Mason spoke briefly on the advantage of voting. Mary Waring attended. The later will address the Lyceum on Sunday, Jan. 30th, on her trip abroad.

Community Center

South Side Community Center is attracting wide interest and attendance from organizations at over the city, including public bystanders, committee, headed by Mrs. Jessie Jones. The immediate activities are under the direction of L. V. Alexia. Organizations at the community center are invited to hold their meetings in its spacious parlors and auditorium. Citizens generally are invited to inspect the work in the community carnival, admission free, will be held at the Community House Saturday, Jan. 22. It will be a big event, with many of the clubs in the city taking part. The public is encouraged to attend the event by Miss Biance Wright, who comes arrange for the recreational institute, Jan. 24-28, evenings. E. T. Attwell, Miss Alice Chiles, George T. Dickerson, and the staff of the workers will take part in the school, which will be the first of its blind in the middle west. The institute will teach free of charge the delegates from all clubs or demonstrations how to create interest in their organizations through the medium of music, dancing and athletics. Register at the Community Center for free avenue, before Jan. 34. There will be no registration fee.
