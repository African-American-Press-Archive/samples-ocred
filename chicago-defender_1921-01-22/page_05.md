STATE INDUSTRIAL COMMISSION
New York, Jan. 21.—A general overhauling of the State Industrial Commission is said to have been planned by republican leaders. It will be introduced at the next legislature meeting.

The program will call for a reduction in the number of commissioners, of which there are at present five, and a wholesale of the office employees, of which there are 1,200. The subdivisions which are slated to abolish are said to be the immigration, education and employment bureau, and the bureau will affect the office in Harlem, located at 184 West 135th street.
