ARTISTIC 'GIRLIE' SHOW AT THE BOOKER WASHINGTON.

With one loner man on the bill at the Booker Washington Theatre this week, this current attraction may well be termed a 'girlie' show. True enough, there are but four of the vivacious vamps on exhibition, but that an sufficiently artful to account for a score of 'skirts'. Sorbra 'Ricks and Ulla Brown,' a sister team, are the headliners. They are bright, speedy and full of snag. Their forte is dancing and they easily with the favor of the audience. The Partisan Toe Dancing of Miss Brown is of rare quality, difficult, but artistically and gracefully executed. Miss Ricks is also an agile dancer and music applauses. Several song numbers are offered among them: 'Royal Garden Blues,' 'Wonderful Doll,' 'My Man Has Wonderful Ways,' 'Ita Dita Doll,' and a lively 'dance' buale. 'Stamping the Blues Away'.

Marguerite Ricks, who is being featured, makes good. She sings and dances her blitheness way into the hearts of her audience and wins fierent applauses. Her song numbers are 'Tulip Time,' 'Cuba' and 'Tell Me Why.' Each number is graced with nimble dance steps and the audience appreciates her art.

Boykins and Williamson are the captain raisers. My Hoykinson is the only man on the program and incidentally the source of laughter. Miss Williamson is graceful, attractive and dance well. She is featuring "The Blues, That Naughty Swains flame to Me." My Hoykinson makes a hit with "A Good Drink Now, Dats! Is Heard to Find." The team puts over a few threads of wit and closes with an imitation trombone and corn sheet.
