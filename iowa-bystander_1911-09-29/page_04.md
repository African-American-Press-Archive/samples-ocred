## College Sources in Politics

To Columbia university belongs the credit of offering the first course designed to give students a practical knowledge of the conduct of political affairs. That institution has asked the bureau of municipal research in New York to give the members of the class the benefit of the experience it gained in the training of the fairs of that city, and the invitation has been accepted. The growing demand for the adoption of the scientific methods of city governments lends support to the view that college men will be in demand in this department of the public service, hence the propriety and usefulness of a university course on the conduct of political affairs indicated by the innovation at Columbia.

Ancient Uses of Bloodnounds
Although the use of bloodnounds for tracking criminals still survives, another ancient use of these dogs seems to have died out. Bloodnounds were at one time often called upon to assist an army in the field, the forces with which the art of Essex suppressed the Irish rebellion in the time of King Bothwath, for instance, being accompanied by 800 dogs. In the Scotch-Irish feuds and the wars between England and Scotland bloodnounds were regularly employed in tracking fugitive warriors, and both Wallace and Bruce were hunted in this manner. Wallace is said to have baffled his pursuers by killing a follower and leaving the corps for the hound to find, while Bruce adopted the less erudite plan of wading some distance down a stream and ascending a tree which overhang the water

## No More Gold Lace for Afghans.

The amoe has published an edited which applies to all parts of Afghanistan, prohibiting the import into the country of all kinds of gold face, including embroidered kullas limgia and embroidered shoes. The amoe is evidently actuated by a desire to prevent his subjects from spending their hard earned money on showy dress. It is the poorer classes who are accidentally addicted to the gold face, the nobility has decided to check. The gold laced coat of the Afghan is decidedly handmade, and although the amoe has acted wisely in bringing into general use clothing less costly, his majesty's orders will doubt less be received by his subjects with rather mixed feelings.
