EVANS HILL, HERMITAGE.
The Thanksgiving holiday was very much enjoyed. Rev. J. L. Webb. The pastor of Evans Hill Baptist Church came out and went rabbit hunting and made the old "Bunnie's go to the brier patch." Rev. W. A. Whittaker also of Nashville, Tenn., spent Thanksgiving at the Hermitage with Mr. Fate Lee they also went rabbit hunting. An entertainment was given at State land school by the teacher, Miss Mexie Evans, Thanksgiving night. Mrs. Ida F. McCauley is at the Vanderbilt bishop, she has recently undergone an operation and is reported nicely at this time. Mrs. Nannie Bell Brooks is able to be up. Mrs. Georgia McClaim has returned from a visit to her mother, at Elkton, Ky. Mrs. Art Hamilton spent several days with her grandmother, Mrs. Nancy Galanes at Sliver Springs. Private Robert Lee Rogers and Jesse Sanders of Camp Sherman, O., have returned to civilian life after spending three months soldier life at camp Sherman. The first golden star to be added to the service flag at the Hermitage is that of Private J. L. Morris. Jr. the son of Mr. John L. Morrison. He had been in the army since Oel. I 1917 and made a good valiant soldier he was first stationed at camp Meade, Md., and then transferred from camp Meade Prince. He was a member of the B. O. Society at Branch Grove. He leaves to mourn their los a father, mother three sisters and three brothers and a host of relatives and friends. One brother is in the service in France. Mr. Jas. Galines spent the night at his sister's Mrs. Ida Mc Cauley. Mr. Price Robertson is very ill.

CARNATION ART CLUB.

The Carneart Art Club held its Thanksgiving meeting at the home of Mrs. Green Hall, 182 Jefferson street.

The reception rooms were decorated with white chrysanthemums and in the dining room where a dainty two course menu was served, white and gold chrysanthemums adorned the table.

(No work was had at this meeting owing to its being a national holiday and each one present enjoyed themselves in a social way. Mrs. J. F. Pierce gave a polished address to us, and thanksgiving and Mrs. E. M. M. Wright favored with a splendid reading. After a real jolly social hour the club adjourned to meet with Mrs. R. F. Wingfield, Dec. 10, 1802 Patterson street. Mrs. James McAdoo was added to the club list.

The visiters present were: Mesdames J. F. Pierce, H. E. Elliott, J. B. Singleton, S. Carter Young, E. M. Wright, Miss Martha Grisham. Club members: Mesdames Wilson, Simmons, Barton, Fields, Freeman, Hall.

SHELBYVILLE.

The community was deeply shocked Saturday afternoon, when the intelligence was spread over the city that Mrs. Gardner, wife of Rev. F. W. Gardner had passed into the great beyond. Sister Gardner was active in every good work, especially was she uniring in her church work. She was a great favorite with the little children. The community sympathizes with the husband in his great bereavement. She was an ideal ministers wife and her many activities will be greatly missed. Rev. J. W. Sebastian, Jr., left Saturday for his work in Ohio. Dr. Randolph and Miss Sallie Temple were united in marriage last week. Nearly all our doctors have married here. Sergeant E. Woolly is repairing the old Woolly home preparatory to moving into it. The influenza is breaking out afresh. Some cases have gone into pneumonia. The total amount raised is $120. For Roger Williams $34,000. If all the obchos do as well, the school will continue to prosper. Brother R. D. Whiteide through his friends raised $19.50 for the state convention. He wishes to thank his friends for their generous help. The public school presented an unusually good program Wednesday night. The attendance was good. The Thanksgiving menu at Turner, was a classy affair and we are glad our people showed their appreciation on the efforts of the domestic science department. The school is getting on a solid foundation notwithstanding the hardships caused by the war and the epidemic. Mr. Allen Scales spent a few days at home. The sick are in nearly every family.

NEWSPAPER MEN COMING

From information given out by the corresponding secretary, a call is soon to be issued by Chairman Joseph L. Jones of the Executive Committee for the annual meeting of the National Negro Press Association, which usually holds its sessions in this city in February. A number of them regard it as the mid-winter session, but their constitution provides that the annual session be held in Nashville in February of each year and that the executive committee meeting be with the Business League in August.

Next year the meeting is supposed to be very largely attended and it is really thought the Assistant Secretary of War, Mr. Emmett J. Scott, will be in attendance at the meeting, as he is a bona fide member and has been very active in helping the newspaper men. The Association, according to reports, has been able to accomplish splendid work during the past year. It was in the Nashville session last February when it was agreed to send a war correspondent to the war zone. This was accomplished, when Mr. Wm. Stephenson landed in France and began to send out his items. But owing to the strict censorship, only general news could be transmitted, and news will have a deal of matter from first hands to give to the readers of the Negro papers during 1919. President C. J. Perry is confident that there will be a larger representation from the easter than in previous years. At the Atlantic City session, which was executive, new life was injected into the Association. The local members are to have a meeting at an early date and begin to arrange for the entertainment of the visiting craftsmen.
