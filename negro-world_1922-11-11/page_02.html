POWER OF THE UNIVERSAL NEGRO IMPROVEMENT ASSOCIATION IS SWEEPING THE COUNTRY

---

LIBERTY HALL. New York Sunday Night. November 5. 1922 - While the enemies of the Universal Negro Improvement Association are busily engaged in disseminating all sorts of pernicious propaganda against the Association and cherishing the false belief that the Association is affected, reports to the contrary come from all parts of the country, indicating that every knock which the Association receives is a boost for it, for the membership increases day by day and the influence of the Association grows stronger and stronger in defiance of all that is said and done against it and its leader Moreover in New York, where the opposition forces are most strongly concentrated, there is an increasing sentiment in favor of the Association, which is evidenced by the tremendous crowds that throng Liberty Hall, especially on Sunday nights, and prove by their unattrained enthusiasm and their moral and financial support that they are in hearty accord with the principles for which the Association stands, and that they are determined to stand by it and for it unto the end.

Ron. R. L. Poston. Secretary-General who has just returned from a trip to certain parts of the South and Middle West, brought glowing reports of the great success which was attending the movement in all the cities which he had visited. If anyone, said he, should happen to feel discouraged about the work of the Universal Negro Improvement Association, he would recommend that such a person take the trip he had just taken. He added further that never in all his life he seen enthusiasm manifested by colored people about a movement as he had seen on his last trip. On his trip he found the enemies of the organization had been at work, but they had failed to do any damage to the Association, because they h. found that the Negro in the Southland is a different Negro from what he was four or five years ago. In conclusion he said that this is the only serious organization that has ever been called into being by colored people's money and that we are dependent upon colored people for everything is causing some people to have sleepless nights. The U. N. L. A. is the only Negro organization in the world. It is accepted as a power, and it is up to us to put our shoulders behind it and put the program over.

Hon. Vernal J. Williams, in an enquired speech delivered with the fire of a man who is convinced of the soundness of the proposition which he is submitting, pointed out that the great issue that transcends and overlays all the other collateral issues that may arise in discussing the future of the Negro was this: "Is Africa going to be redeemed and be independent?" This great overarching question, Mr. Williams declared, has been brought before the world and before international public opinion since the advent of the Universal Negro Improvement Association. The program of the organization, he said, could not be compared with any other Negro organization, in that it had carried the struggle of the black peoples of the world into the great unlimited sphere of governmental power. The purpose of other Negro organizations was simply to contend for certain limited rights where the Negroes are, but they forgot that wherever there are certain corresponding obligations, and for any people to maintain their rights they must live up to the corresponding obligation of backing those rights. In order, therefore, for the Negro to contend for and maintain rights, he must get himself first of all into a governmental and political status where he can defend and protect those rights for which he is contending. For the Negro to enjoy rights or opportunities at all he must have behind him a government strong enough, big enough, magnificent enough, respected enough to demand, maintain and protect those institutions of liberty and that modicum of a free existence that he is seeking. The paramount question, he said, for us to decide was Africa, and if we lost sight of it, then we are going to certainly decline, because Africa is the key that is opening the door of success to all the nations, and if the U. N. E. A. continues this fight for Africa, it is going to gather into its fold every honest, righteous-thinking black man and woman, regardless of where they come from or where they are going.

The Progress of Races

The true progress of a race depends upon its own energy, its own effort, its own initiative. The race that has no initiative has absolutely no future. Look at things as they are. In this Western world we find the race without any initiative, we find the race without a vision but that supplied by the Universal Negro Improvement Association. In trying to place this initiative, in trying to place this vision, what do we come in contact with? Because of the lack of understanding among the races we come in direct contact with the enemies who seek to destroy our vision and to deprive us of this outlook. The Universal Negro Improvement Association finds itself in this position at this time. In nearly every section of this Western world, and for that matter in every popular section of the world where Negroes live, there springs up a voluntary opposition to our program, not so much from among others, because, as I have said before, we know our enemies among the people that we have to fight in the great conflict to reach destiny. But what I am speaking of is this opposition that springs from within, wherein this community as well as in this country and all over the world we have men of our race who have made it their duty to place as many barriers as possible in the way of the Universal Negro Improvement Association to prevent this vision taking hold of all the people and directing the people in one direction.

Who would believe it that in this community—and because we are so near home we will speak of this community, and what is true of this is true of all—that men take special pains in misrepresenting the aims and objects of this Association to the powers that be; to the forces that control, because of their selfishness in believing that through a movement so gigantic, so attractive to which millions of people rush, will come a recognition that probably would be justly merited that would not be attracted their way. Hon R. L. Boston was the first speaker and said I am just back from a trip to Cleveland, Ohio, and New Orleans. La also, and it was a wonderful trip. It any one should happen to feel discouraged about the work of the Universal Negro Improvement Association I would recommend that such a person take the trip that I have just taken. Wherever I have gone I have met with audiences just such as this, and there has been an admission attached to the meetings that I have adored, yet the crowds have been just like this crowd in Liberty Hall tonight. I have never seen in all my life the enthusiasm manifested by colored people about a movement as I have seen on this last trip. We spoke twice in Cleveland—in the afternoon at the Lanes A M E Church, where the building was packed, and that night we spoke at the regular hall of the association, and that was jammed. In Cincinnati the Hon Mr Ware, president of that division, had secured the largest hall in the city, and we were not to fill this cuilding—a thing no other organization in Cleveland, or no two organizations combined, could have thought of doing. After leaving Cincinnati we took a most interesting trip down through the Southland to Louisiana. I presume you have heard already of the meeting of His Excellency in Raleigh, N.C. where the white papers for the first time in the State of North Carolina carried front page articles in regard to His Excellency the Provisional President of Africa. Reporters came from cities far and near throughout the State to report those meetings, and the Universal Negro Improvement Association in the State of North Carolina has been advanced 100 per cent.

Then I went down to New Orleans, where I learned for the first time what a person must really put up with if he is connected with an organization such as this. Before we reached New Orleans the largest hall that the colored people could secure there had been sold out days before we reached there. DRAKE AND WALKER AT THE LAFAYETTE THEATRE IN "BOMBAY GIRLS"

By LEUBRIE

After treating their patrons to two white companies the Coleman Brothers have returned to colored musical comedy, and this return is celebrated with the famous Drake and Walker Company in Bombay Girls,' also Burt Earle and his girls and other big acts showing this week

Drake and Walker are known to theatregoers all over the country as two of the funniest comedians in the business. The company has a galaxy of beautiful and talented girls who will fill in two hours and a half of the liveliest and cleanest entertainment.

The Coleman Brothers have been lending a little variety to their program with the view of ascertaining just what the public likes best. Our observation has led us to believe that the colored musical comedy goes biggest at the Lafayette.

We understand that some splendid companies have been booked to appear at this popular house, and the indications are that the Lafayette will be a live spot right up through the holidays. There is a midnight show every Friday night and on Sunday there are continuous shows from 3 to 11 p.m.

CALVERY.CHURCH IN
HARLEM FOR NEGROES

Transfer of Pioneer M. E. Edifice to Salem Congregation Arranged Last Spring

By an arrangement closed last spring the edifice of the Calvary Methodist Episcopal Church, at the corner of Seventh avenue and 129th street, is to be conveyed to the Salem Church, a colored unit of the Methodist Episcopal Church, for $288,500, when the Calvary congregation build their new church in another section, perhaps Washington Heights.

Calvary Church was built some thirty-five years ago, as a pioneer in upper Harlem, its anniversary having been celebrated last week, when all the old living pastors attended the ceremonies, its present pastor being the Rev William P Odell. The pastor at the time of the building was the Rev. Frank Mason North, now secretary of the Foreign Mission Society, which will assist the Salary congregation in paying for Calvary Church. The latter congregation is now housed at the southwest corner of 133d street and Lenox avenue, where it owns a plot 100x125, which is under negotiation for a sale.

The Progress of Races

The true progress of a race depends upon its own energy, its own effort, its own initiative. The race that has no initiative has absolutely no future. Look at things as they are. In this Western world we find the race without any initiative, we find the race without a vision but that supplied by the Universal Negro Improvement Association. In trying to place this initiative, in trying to place this vision, what do we come in contact with? Because of the lack of understanding among the races we come in direct contact with the enemies who seek to destroy our vision and to deprive us of this outlook. The Universal Negro Improvement Association finds itself in this position at this time. In nearly every section of this Western world, and for that matter in every popular section of the world where Negroes live, there springs up a voluntary opposition to our program, not so much from among others, because, as I have said before, we know our enemies among the people that we have to fight in the great conflict to reach destiny. But what I am speaking of is this opposition that springs from within, wherein this community as well as in this country and all over the world we have men of our race who have made it their duty to place as many barriers as possible in the way of the Universal Negro Improvement Association to prevent this vision taking hold of all the people and directing the people in one direction. It any one should happen to feel discouraged about the work of the Universal Negro Improvement Association I would recommend that such a person take the trip that I have just taken. In Cincinnati the Hon Mr Ware, president of that division, had secured the largest hall in the city, and we were not to fill this cuilding—a thing no other organization in Cleveland, or no two organizations combined, could have thought of doing. After leaving Cincinnati we took a most interesting trip down through the Southland to Louisiana. I presume you have heard already of the meeting of His Excellency in Raleigh, N.C. where the white papers for the first time in the State of North Carolina carried front page articles in regard to His Excellency the Provisional President of Africa. Reporters came from cities far and near throughout the State to report those meetings, and the Universal Negro Improvement Association in the State of North Carolina has been advanced 100 per cent. Then I went down to New Orleans, where I learned for the first time what a person must really put up with if he is connected with an organization such as this. Before we reached New Orleans the largest hall that the colored people could secure there had been sold out days before we reached there. CALVERY.CHURCH IN HARLEM FOR NEGROES

Transfer of Pioneer M. E. Edifice to Salem Congregation Arranged Last Spring By an arrangement closed last spring the edifice of the Calvary Methodist Episcopal Church, at the corner of Seventh avenue and 129th street, is to be conveyed to the Salem Church, a colored unit of the Methodist Episcopal Church, for $288,500, when the Calvary congregation build their new church in another section, perhaps Washington Heights. Calvary Church was built some thirty-five years ago, as a pioneer in upper Harlem, its anniversary having been celebrated last week, when all the old living pastors attended the ceremonies, its present pastor being the Rev William P Odell. The pastor at the time of the building was the Rev. Frank Mason North, now secretary of the Foreign Mission Society, which will assist the Salary congregation in paying for Calvary Church. The latter congregation is now housed at the southwest corner of 133d street and Lenox avenue, where it owns a plot 100x125, which is under negotiation for a sale.
