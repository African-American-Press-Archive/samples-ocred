HOWARD ELEVEN SWAMPS NORTH CAROLINA AGRICULTURAL AND TECHNICAL COLLEGE SCORE OF 40—0

WASHINGTON, D. C., Oct. 25.—The Howard University football eleven in its second game of the season, played Saturday afternoon, October 21, 1922, o the university campus, swamped the North Carolina Agricultural and Technical Colege team, of Greenaboro, N. C. by a score of 40 to 0. The "Blue and White" eleven outclassed the visitors in every department of the game and won about as it pleased.

The game began with Howard kicking off to the Carolinians. After about three minutes of play, Conte, left half-back for Howard, recovered a fumble made by the Carolinians with the ball on their 25-yard line and sprinted away for a touchdown. The attempted drop kick by Doneghy failed. Howard kicked off again and downed the A. and T man on the run-back a. About the same distance from the North Carolinians' goal line as the position of the ball when the costly fumble was made. Following a couple of plays A. and T. kicked out, but the ball was brought up some distance by Howard and after a series of line plunges was worked across the North Carolinians' goal line, being carried across by Doneghy. Howard's right halfback. This time Doneghy's drop kick registered for the additional point.

Howard kicked off to A. and T. for the third time in the first quarter, the ball going all the way down the field across the North Carolinians' goal line. The Howard boys followed up the ball so speedily that before an A. and T man could recover it Doneghy fell upon the ball for another touchdown. Doneghy tried another drop kick for the extra point but failed.

Beginning with the second quarter the North Carolina boys started out in what seemed would be fruitful style. By the use of a trick delayed pass which caused the player taking the ball to use the opposite direction from

his interference they succeeded in making a couple of good gains. After making use of the play twice, however, the Blue and White eleven caught on to t and successfully broke it up. To - the end of the quarter the North Carolina boys by means of a number of penalties got far down into Howard's territory. After being held for three downs a placement kick was tried, which was blocked and recovered by "Bull Dog" Williams, Howard's famous left end and captain of the team, who in his old style galloped down the field for some 10 yards to A. and T.'s goal for a touchdown. Doneghy by drop kick secured the additional point.

With about six minutes to play, after having received the kick-off from the Carolinians and bringing the ball back up the field by a number of line drives to the visitors' 35-yard line, Coach Morrison sent in his second team. The North Carolina boys again attempted their delayed pass play, which, as in the case of its use against the first team, momentarily nonplussed Howard's second team. But after permitting the A. and T. boys to gain two first downs by this play, the second team boys caught on and broke it up. When the referee's white's blew announcing the end of the first half the ball was in Howard's possession.

The third quarter was uneventful, being spent mainly in punting from one team to the other. The fourth quarter netted Howard two touchdowns, one being secured by Melton, left halfback, who also assisted in getting the back additional point by receiving the well directed forward pass from Carter, Howard's star quarterback. The other touchdown was secured by Cornish, left tackle, who intercepted a forward pass tried by the Carolinians and paced away for their goal. The additional point in this case was made by Carter, quarterback, by the use of a drop kick.

Much surprise was expressed by the large crowd of spectators that the Howard eleven showed such amazing activity in the game after having ap-

peared so slow in its first game, having won its first game by a score of only 8 to 4. The showing of the team, however, was gratifying to its coaches and followers. After the mediocre opening game the "Blue and White" came to life and played with plenty of dash and precision.

PROF. SUAREZ SPEAKS AT 135TH ST. "Y"

Dr Bernardo Ruiz Suares will speak at the West 135th Street T. M. C. A. on Tuesday evening. November 7 at 8 p.m.

Dr Suares is the author of several books on Negro life in the Spanish countries, and his latest book, "The Color Question in the Two Americas," has attracted wide attention in both North and South America.

We are expecting a large and appreciative attendance at the lecture by Dr. Suares.

Howard's right halfback. This time Doneghy's drop kick registered for the additional point. Howard kicked off to A. and T. for the third time in the first quarter, the ball going all the way down the field across the North Carolinians' goal line. The Howard boys followed up the ball so speedily that before an A. and T man could recover it Doneghy fell upon the ball for another touchdown. Doneghy tried another drop kick for the extra point but failed. Beginning with the second quarter the North Carolina boys started out in what seemed would be fruitful style. By the use of a trick delayed pass which caused the player taking the ball to use the opposite direction from

his interference they succeeded in making a couple of good gains. After making use of the play twice, however, the Blue and White eleven caught on to t and successfully broke it up. To - the end of the quarter the North Carolina boys by means of a number of penalties got far down into Howard's territory. After being held for three downs a placement kick was tried, which was blocked and recovered by "Bull Dog" Williams, Howard's famous left end and captain of the team, who in his old style galloped down the field for some 10 yards to A. and T.'s goal for a touchdown. Doneghy by drop kick secured the additional point. With about six minutes to play, after having received the kick-off from the Carolinians and bringing the ball back up the field by a number of line drives to the visitors' 35-yard line, Coach Morrison sent in his second team. The North Carolina boys again attempted their delayed pass play, which, as in the case of its use against the first team, momentarily nonplussed Howard's second team. But after permitting the A. and T. boys to gain two first downs by this play, the second team boys caught on and broke it up. When the referee's white's blew announcing the end of the first half the ball was in Howard's possession. The third quarter was uneventful, being spent mainly in punting from one team to the other. The fourth quarter netted Howard two touchdowns, one being secured by Melton, left halfback, who also assisted in getting the back additional point by receiving the well directed forward pass from Carter, Howard's star quarterback. The other touchdown was secured by Cornish, left tackle, who intercepted a forward pass tried by the Carolinians and paced away for their goal. The additional point in this case was made by Carter, quarterback, by the use of a drop kick. Much surprise was expressed by the large crowd of spectators that the Howard eleven showed such amazing activity in the game after having ap-

peared so slow in its first game, having won its first game by a score of only 8 to 4. The showing of the team, however, was gratifying to its coaches and followers.
