The case against the Honorable Marc Garvey, Ellie Garcia and George Tobias of the Black Star Line for alleged misuse of the United States mails will be called some time this month in New York. WELLS SAYS LEAGUE
IS ONLY MAKESHIFT
By ROBERT J. PREW

LONDON. Nov 3 — Unified world control of world affairs is the foremost plea advanced by H. G. Wells in an election address which will be published tomorrow. Wells is labor candidate for Parliament from London University. He says.

"It is plain common sense that world control in international affairs must represent all the main powers of the world. The present League of Nations has neither prestige nor authority to handle the greater international issues of today.

"We have to do what we can with and for the League of Nations, and particularly we have to sustain its world labor organization, but we must never forget how provisional and experimental a body it is and the urgent need in which it stands for drastic reconstruction that will give Germany Russia. Turkey and other excluded powers a sense of equal and honorable co-operation."

MARY AUSTIN TO SPEAK

AT 135TH ST. LIBRARY

Patrons of the library will be glad to hear that the book evenings started last year are to be resumed this fall. The first will be Wednesday, November 15, and the speaker will be Mira Mary Austin, who will talk on the "Community Theatre." Mira Austin will speak at 8 15, and the guests are asked to remain afterwards for a social hour.

Mira Austin, one of the foremost women novelists of the country, has spent a great part of her life in the West in the unattended lands about the Sierra Nevada mountains and the desert edges, among the Indians, and has written great deal on Indian life and customs. She is not only an author but a playwright and lecturer Her well-known play, "The Arrowmaker." is one of her best. Some of her other books are "California, the Land of the Sun," a book of travel, and "The Ford" and "No 26 Jayne Street" both novels. You will miss an unusually interesting program if you do not hear Mira Austin.

WELLS SAYS LEAGUE IS ONLY MAKESHIFT By ROBERT J. PREW

LONDON. Nov 3 — Unified world control of world affairs is the foremost plea advanced by H. G. Wells in an election address which will be published tomorrow. Wells is labor candidate for Parliament from London University. He says. "It is plain common sense that world control in international affairs must represent all the main powers of the world. The present League of Nations has neither prestige nor authority to handle the greater international issues of today. "We have to do what we can with and for the League of Nations, and particularly we have to sustain its world labor organization, but we must never forget how provisional and experimental a body it is and the urgent need in which it stands for drastic reconstruction that will give Germany Russia. Turkey and other excluded powers a sense of equal and honorable co-operation."

MARY AUSTIN TO SPEAK

AT 135TH ST. LIBRARY Patrons of the library will be glad to hear that the book evenings started last year are to be resumed this fall. The first will be Wednesday, November 15, and the speaker will be Mira Mary Austin, who will talk on the "Community Theatre." Mira Austin will speak at 8 15, and the guests are asked to remain afterwards for a social hour. Mira Austin, one of the foremost women novelists of the country, has spent a great part of her life in the West in the unattended lands about the Sierra Nevada mountains and the desert edges, among the Indians, and has written great deal on Indian life and customs. She is not only an author but a playwright and lecturer Her well-known play, "The Arrowmaker." is one of her best. Some of her other books are "California, the Land of the Sun," a book of travel, and "The Ford" and "No 26 Jayne Street" both novels. You will miss an unusually interesting program if you do not hear Mira Austin.
