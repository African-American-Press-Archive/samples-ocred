Henderson; Mendesias M. Allen, Cornelia Henderson, T. P. Wright, Eliza Freeman, P. V. Wright, Christina Wesley, Revs. J. E. Jones, T. A. Reid and J. W. Jarvis. Cheapest Light and Fuel

The U. S. Bureau of Standards announces, in an official bulletin, that the mantle gas light is the cheapest of all house lights.

The Bureau's tests show that the antiquated flat flame burner uses up five times as much gas as the mantle burner to produce the same amount of light.

The tests also show, that for the same amount of light, flat flame lighting costs about four times as much as mantle lighting, including cost of mantles.

Since "candle power" is useless in mantle lighting, isn't it perfectly plain that the most economical household would save money with "heat unit" gas and mantles for all light-

KEEP FRONT OF YOUR RESIDENCE CLEAN

There are certain sections of the city, and especially on the South Side, that resemble some of the back streets in southern cities. On Wabash avenue, in many blocks the grass is so high that it resembles early wheat fields. One day last week a little boy was thought to have lost. The police were called and they looked all over on the South Side for him. Finally, they stumbled in the grass and there lay the boy asleep, covered by grass, in the front yard where the mother and father live. The father too lay a cut the grass or too stingy to have it cut. On some the best paved streets, where members of the Race are allowed to rent, they keep their pavements dirty and nasty, and the front steps and flagging the same way. Grass on the pavements, dirty curtains and shades in many houses tell that that is where Race people live. On some blocks on Wabash avenue, for instance, the Fifty-third street block is an example, where in the Thirty-second street block and further north it is a shame and a disgrace at the way in which the front yards and pavements look. The Chicago Defender urges that more care be given to homes and that they should be so clean that they are out that there lives members of the Race, whether it be on Armour avenue or St. Lawrence avenue.

Cheapest Light and Fuel

The U. S. Bureau of Standards announces, in an official bulletin, that the mantle gas light is the cheapest of all house lights. The Bureau's tests show that the antiquated flat flame burner uses up five times as much gas as the mantle burner to produce the same amount of light. The tests also show, that for the same amount of light, flat flame lighting costs about four times as much as mantle lighting, including cost of mantles. KEEP FRONT OF YOUR RESIDENCE CLEAN

There are certain sections of the city, and especially on the South Side, that resemble some of the back streets in southern cities. On Wabash avenue, in many blocks the grass is so high that it resembles early wheat fields. One day last week a little boy was thought to have lost. The police were called and they looked all over on the South Side for him. Finally, they stumbled in the grass and there lay the boy asleep, covered by grass, in the front yard where the mother and father live. The father too lay a cut the grass or too stingy to have it cut. On some the best paved streets, where members of the Race are allowed to rent, they keep their pavements dirty and nasty, and the front steps and flagging the same way. Grass on the pavements, dirty curtains and shades in many houses tell that that is where Race people live. On some blocks on Wabash avenue, for instance, the Fifty-third street block is an example, where in the Thirty-second street block and further north it is a shame and a disgrace at the way in which the front yards and pavements look. The Chicago Defender urges that more care be given to homes and that they should be so clean that they are out that there lives members of the Race, whether it be on Armour avenue or St. Lawrence avenue.
