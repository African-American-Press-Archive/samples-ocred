THE BRAVEST OF THE BRAVE

"Oh, he's a nigger!"

Sure, but henceforth and forever let there be no doubt in our minds that courage has anything to do with skin.

As the reports from Carrizal filter in, every item of it shows that the negro troopers of Troops C and K, U. S. A., are just as much entitled to the respect of patriotic Americans as the heroes of Lexington, the Alamo or the Little Big Horn.

The odds were at least ten to one—perhaps fifty to one—against which these gallant black boys fought!

If they are not in history the bravest of the brave, they are as brave as the bravest!

Hats off, fellers, to Troops C and K, Tenth regiment, U. S. A.

—El Paso Democrat.

Vacation—Everybody Needs a Vacation
—How to Take a Vacation

Do you know, Mr. Reader, Mr. City Dweller, that you need a vacation? Do you know that for the good of your physical condition—for the improvement of your moral condition and for the broadening and illuminating of your mental condition, you should change, at least, once a year from your daily strenuous labor to a period of relaxation, change of scenes to different scenery, that you should meet new people, encounter new surroundings and conditions; that you should get out of the hot flats, apartments, narrow streets and alleys, from dusty and unhealthy surroundings, and go to the country—get out into the open—go to the seashore, to the shore of the lake, enjoy the bathing beaches, commune with nature and with nature's God. We all must have rest; we must go in or out, take it anyway you please, but we must lay up for rest and repairs.

If you feel that you are not able to leave the cury, hike out to the parks, to the woods, into the country. The municipal pier at the foot of Grand avenue is a fine, cool spot for you on a hot day; learn to swim; go and enjoy the bathing beaches. Why not arrange to have a week-end vacation? Chicago is surrounded and filled, one might say, with many beautiful parks, quiet places for rest and comfort, plenty of good water and splendid shade. Why not spend a time each week, take a vacation with a few friends, or just the children and their little friends, and quietly take your rest in one of the many beautiful parks instead of sitting around in the doorway, out on the doorsteps, hanging with head and half of body out of the window, wearing yourself out fighting flies, bugs and other insects? You could take your lunch or dinner to the park and enjoy a good, cool repast and thus find yourself better prepared for the duties of your occupation the end of your vacation.

BIG FINANCIAL MOVE

Of interest financially to the Race is in the announcement of the formation of a syndicate headed by W. H. C. Brown, investment banker, Washington, D. C., and Brown and Stevens, bankers, Philadelphia, Pa., and others, which syndicate has underwritten the entire new issue of $50,000 capital and surplus of Standard Life Insurance company. When this company, which is the only old-guard reserve life insurance company in the United States, the Race, was first organized it was necessary to secure the aid of white banking institutions to help finance it. It is a signal advance in the history of the financial affairs of the Race that a group of Race financial institutions can undertake and carry forward a project involving so large an amount of money. It is very creditable to these financial institutions that their resources, enable them with care on such undertakings, fraught with care by their partners and which will result profitably to themselves, the public and the company. This stock will be offered to the public by the syndicate and an opportunity given to participate, in this great organization.

Vacation—Everybody Needs a Vacation —How to Take a Vacation Do you know, Mr. Reader, Mr. City Dweller, that you need a vacation? Do you know that for the good of your physical condition—for the improvement of your moral condition and for the broadening and illuminating of your mental condition, you should change, at least, once a year from your daily strenuous labor to a period of relaxation, change of scenes to different scenery, that you should meet new people, encounter new surroundings and conditions; that you should get out of the hot flats, apartments, narrow streets and alleys, from dusty and unhealthy surroundings, and go to the country—get out into the open—go to the seashore, to the shore of the lake, enjoy the bathing beaches, commune with nature and with nature's God. We all must have rest; we must go in or out, take it anyway you please, but we must lay up for rest and repairs.

If you feel that you are not able to leave the cury, hike out to the parks, to the woods, into the country. The municipal pier at the foot of Grand avenue is a fine, cool spot for you on a hot day; learn to swim; go and enjoy the bathing beaches. Why not arrange to have a week-end vacation? Chicago is surrounded and filled, one might say, with many beautiful parks, quiet places for rest and comfort, plenty of good water and splendid shade. Why not spend a time each week, take a vacation with a few friends, or just the children and their little friends, and quietly take your rest in one of the many beautiful parks instead of sitting around in the doorway, out on the doorsteps, hanging with head and half of body out of the window, wearing yourself out fighting flies, bugs and other insects? You could take your lunch or dinner to the park and enjoy a good, cool repast and thus find yourself better prepared for the duties of your occupation the end of your vacation.
