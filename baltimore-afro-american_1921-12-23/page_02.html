FRIDAY, DEC. 23, 1921

Mrs. Theresa Cain

On Sunday, December 11, 1821, the funeral of Mrs. Theresa Cain was held at Elenzer Baptist Church. East 42rd street. The sermon was preached by Rev. R. A. Green, of St. Matthew M. E. Church. The body was in charge of North Star Tab. No. 57. A. U. O. of Moses. The other orders that officiated were Doresc Tab No. 11. G. U. O. of Nazaretes, Union Star Court of Calabria, K. of P. Royal Arch No. 6. G. D. C. K. of P. Women's Relief Codes No. 11. Logan Post, G. A. R. Queen and Juveniles Tab. F. of P. King Memphis Tab. No. 57. W. O. of Moses. Missionary Elenzer Lant Church. Missionary Circle of Elenzer Baptist Church, and the Young Ladies Industrial Creed: Club and the Helping Hand Social of North Baltimore. There were beautiful floral designs from the many orders and friends. Mrs. Robert Ebbott, internment in M. Auburn Cemetery.

Often I think of you, dear husband. And my heart is sad with pain. All this earth would be a heaven. Could I hear your voice again.

Days of sadness still come over me. Tears of sorrow silently flow. Fond memories keeps me near three The Heaven claimed you three years ago.

Jay his loving Wife.

PEAKER—in sad but loving remembrance of our dear mother, Emma Peaker (nee Marshall), who was laid to rest one year ago December 19, 1920.
Loved in life; in death remembered.

You would have been home long ago.

I did not know the pain you bore.

I did not see you die.

Gone in the best of her days
Blighted in womanhood's bloom
Taken from the hearts that loved
her:
To sleep in the silent tomb,
One year has passed away my
heart is sore;

As time flies on I miss my dear mother more.

She sleeps—I leave her in peace to rest;

The parting was painful, but God knows best.

Yes, the dreadful message came to me, our dear mother is dead.

Her smiling face on earth no more I will see.

Her spirit is at rest.

She hone gone to be with Jesus in the land of the blest.

When all the world is cheerful And smiles are on each bow.

Oh, blame me not for weeping For I have no mother now.
